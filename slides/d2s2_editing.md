# proofreading

- Proofreading is an important skill that all writers need to learn.
- Proofreading is about:
- checking the spelling and grammar
- identifying if the idea or argument has been conveyed succinctly
- reorganising and simplifying the expression of ideas
- clarifying the structure and organisation
- avoiding ambiguity and repetition
- finding any other changes to make that ease the reader's task

---

# proofreading

- Proofreading is usually done on paper although it can be done on PDFs or as comments in a Word document
- The output of proofreading is a marked up document. Example mark-up symbols are available online https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_proofreader%27s_marks
- Proofreading is a separate activity from producing text.
- When done well, proofreading can contribute significantly to the success of any academic piece of writing.

---

# reverse mind mapping

- usually, you create a mind map before writing
- but sometimes, a paragraph or section is confused or confusing and needs rewriting
- try this:
  - produce a mind map from the text, reorganise the map, then rewrite from the map
  - alternatively, produce bullet points from the text (at the top of the section) then reorder, revise and when happy drop back down to para text

---

# introducing the proofreading club

- Here’s a club you can set up with your friends:
- Step 1: negotiate when and how often to meet. Two hours duration is usually enough
- Step 2: Two or three days prior, the assigned member sends out his writing draft to the rest of the group
- Step 3: Other members spend 30 - 60 mins proofreading the draft, marking it up
- Step 4: The meeting - take turns to make suggestions, and discuss as a group.
